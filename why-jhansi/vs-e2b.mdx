Both provide sandboxes for agent-generated code. They differ primarily in operating model and intended scope.

This is a positioning guide, not a security benchmark. Capabilities change — validate both products against current documentation before making an architectural decision.

## Comparison

| Dimension | jhansi.io | E2B |
|-----------|-----------|-----|
| Primary posture | A small, self-operated execution layer. | A mature sandbox platform and cloud service. |
| Default experience | Run and operate the runtime in your environment. | Use hosted sandboxes through an API key and SDK. |
| Customer infrastructure | Self-hosting is central to the product direction. | Offers bring-your-own-cloud options. |
| Maturity | Early stage, actively developed. | Production-ready with a broad customer base. |

## When jhansi.io is the better fit

- The runtime must run inside your own infrastructure.
- You need execution close to private systems or internal networks.
- You want to inspect and control the execution layer itself.

## When E2B may be the better fit

- You want a managed service with no infrastructure to operate.
- You need a production-ready platform today.
- Your data and compliance requirements permit a third-party execution plane.
